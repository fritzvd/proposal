Davies offers an example from her own research in an educational context where boys who have been reprimanded by their teacher for fighting take up the category of 'naughty boy'. While the teacher imposes the category 'naughty boy' by terminating the fight and reproaching them for inappropriate behaviour, the boys engage in a reworking of this discourse's power. They wilfully take up this positioning by ceasing their fight and submitting to the teacher's chastisement while refusing to enact feelings of shame and a desire for reform the category presumes. Instead, the boys appropriate 'naughty boy' by laughing and smirking behind the teacher's back and continuing to misbehave in another class. Through their appropriation of the 'naughty boy' category, these boys can be seen to exercise agency.
