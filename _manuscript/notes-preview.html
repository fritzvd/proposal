Hypotheses to work with or maxims and possible case studies

  • Digital divide discourse is not advancing the closing of the divide

  • Closing the digital divide is not alleviating poverty
  • Big tech’s push for the next billion users is unethically preying on the new and inexperienced users
  • Improving privacy literacy will improve a users engagement with platforms
  • Privacy and security are low priority
  • The global south is likely to fall prey to exposurism or pilotism, the idea that companies use interventions and experiments to test a theory and increase their PR by showing that they are involved in “community efforts”
    • Why is it likely to fall prey? Or are they able to rise above.
    • shown by humanitarianism in Technocolonialism
  • AI hype and positivity nudges people to put their trust in platforms, but support will lack in the future
    • who will benefit (Houston and Erdelez 2002)
  • Technosolutionism puts too much trust in AI and will ask from new users to engage with these tools. These tools are not free and cost a lot to maintain
  • Offline-first education tools.
  • Data protection regulation is not being enforced and falls short.
  • Machine learning’s biases are implicit but not without consequence
    • example DRC refugees: Madianou (2024) p.140. When the decision is made by the machine without any clear explanation about the criteria used, then it is harder to challenge [.. exclusion].
    • Example Australia robodebt.
